Open Voice Project

Building the first open TTRPG audio dataset

Tabletop RPG sessions are one of the richest forms of natural multi-speaker conversation — players narrate, argue, joke, and perform character voices, often for hours at a time. Yet no open dataset of this audio exists for researchers to study.

The Open Voice Project records TTRPG sessions from Discord with explicit consent from every participant, producing per-speaker audio tracks that are transcribed, reviewed, and released as a public dataset under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Why this matters

  • No community-contributed TTRPG audio dataset exists — everything is scraped from YouTube
  • Per-speaker tracks eliminate the need for diarization, enabling cleaner ASR training
  • Players alter their voices for characters, creating a uniquely challenging benchmark
  • Multi-system coverage (D&D, Pathfinder, Call of Cthulhu, and more) — not just one show
  • Consent-first collection — every participant explicitly agrees before recording begins

How it works

A Discord bot joins your voice channel and records each speaker as a separate audio track. Before recording starts, every participant must consent via a simple button prompt. When the session ends, audio is converted to FLAC, pseudonymized, and uploaded to secure storage.

No one is recorded without their explicit permission. Participants can decline and remain in the voice channel — their audio is simply not captured. Consent can be withdrawn before the data is published.

What gets collected

  • Per-speaker FLAC audio (48kHz, lossless)
  • Session metadata (game system, campaign, duration)
  • Consent records with pseudonymized participant IDs
  • Optional GM session notes

Discord usernames and IDs are never included in the public dataset. Speaker identities are replaced with deterministic pseudonyms so researchers can track the same voice across sessions without knowing who they are.

The dataset

We're in early collection. The dataset will be published on HuggingFace once we have enough validated sessions. Everything is released under CC BY-SA 4.0 — free to use, share, and build on.

Contribute

If you run a TTRPG group on Discord and want to contribute your sessions to the dataset, we'd love to hear from you. The bot handles everything — consent, recording, upload. Your group just plays as normal.

Technical details

The collector bot is written in Rust using Songbird for voice capture with Discord's DAVE end-to-end encryption protocol. Audio is captured as raw PCM via the VoiceTick event interface, converted to FLAC on session end, and uploaded to S3-compatible object storage.
